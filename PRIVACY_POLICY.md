## 4. DEFINITIONS & TERMINOLOGY

### 4.1 Key Definitions

**Personal Data:**
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual. Examples include:

- Name, email address, phone number
- Date of birth, age
- Home address or location
- Device information
- IP address
- Any information that can identify you or be linked to you

**Processing:**
Any operation performed on personal data, including:

- Collection
- Recording
- Organization
- Storage
- Alteration
- Retrieval
- Use
- Transmission
- Deletion

**Data Subject:**
The individual to whom personal data relates. **You are the data subject** in this relationship.

**Data Controller:**
The organization that decides how and why personal data is processed. **Dayo is the data controller** for your data.

**Data Processor:**
The organization that processes data on behalf of the controller. **Supabase, Google, and Facebook are data processors** for Dayo.

**Consent:**
Your voluntary, informed agreement to processing. Consent must be:

- ✅ Freely given (not coerced)
- ✅ Specific (about specific processing activities)
- ✅ Informed (you understand what you're agreeing to)
- ✅ Unambiguous (a clear affirmative action)

**Legitimate Interest:**
A reason for processing that benefits Dayo or society. Examples include:

- ✅ Service improvement
- ✅ Fraud prevention
- ✅ Account security
- ✅ Legal compliance

**Special Categories:**
Sensitive data requiring extra protection:

- Location data (GPS coordinates)
- Health information (if shared in posts)
- Biometric data (if any)

**Data Breach:**
Unauthorized access, disclosure, or loss of personal data.

**Right to Be Forgotten:**
Your right to have personal data deleted or anonymized.

**Data Portability:**
Your right to receive data in a standard format.

```

18:02:00 - 2 minutes later
- Location updates again

20:00:00 - Match ends

- Location no longer accessible

- Privacy: No history of where players went after match
- Security: Cannot be used to track users after match
- Data minimization: Only kept as long as needed
```

4. Preserve evidence (for investigation and legal)
5. Notify leadership and legal team

**Investigation Phase (24-48 hours):**

1. Determine scope (how many users affected)
2. Identify data breached (which fields/information)
3. Identify cause (how did breach occur)
4. Identify attacker (if possible)
5. Assess impact (severity level)

**Notification Phase (Within 72 hours):**

1. Notify affected users (via email to registered email)
2. Notify authorities if legally required
3. Notify credit bureaus if payment data exposed
4. Publish public notice on website

**Remediation Phase (Ongoing):**

1. Close vulnerability that enabled breach
2. Implement fixes and security patches
3. Reset passwords for affected users (if necessary)
4. Offer credit monitoring (if payment data exposed)
