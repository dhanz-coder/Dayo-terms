## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY & PRINCIPLES

### 1.1 Our Community Values

Dayo is built on these core principles:

**🤝 Respect**

- Treat all players with dignity and kindness
- Celebrate diversity (gender, age, skill level, background)
- Disagree respectfully without personal attacks
- Support fellow athletes

**⚽ Fair Play**

- Competition with integrity
- No cheating or match-fixing
- Honest skill ratings and attendance
- Sportsmanship above winning

**🛡️ Safety**

- Safe environment for all ages (13+)
- Zero tolerance for predatory behavior
- Protect vulnerable community members
- Report unsafe behavior immediately

**🎯 Transparency**

- Clear rules that everyone understands
- Transparent moderation decisions
- Opportunity to appeal
- Learning from mistakes

**🌍 Inclusivity**

- Welcome players of all skill levels
- Support beginner-friendly spaces
- Celebrate all achievements
- No discrimination based on identity

### 1.2 What These Guidelines Cover

These Community Guidelines apply to:

- ✅ All content you post (text, photos, videos)
- ✅ All messages and communications with other users
- ✅ Your profile, bio, and personal information
- ✅ How you organize and participate in matches
- ✅ How you represent yourself and your clubs
- ✅ Any commercial activity (coaching, services)
- ✅ How you interact with our moderation team

**We believe in:**

- ✅ Clear rules so everyone knows expectations
- ✅ Fair, consistent enforcement
- ✅ Education over punishment (when possible)

- ✅ Opportunity to improve and rejoin community

**We don't believe in:**

- ❌ Arbitrary or biased enforcement
- ❌ Secret rules or surprise bans
- ❌ Permanent bans for minor mistakes
- ❌ Moderator bias or favoritism
- ❌ Public shaming or spectacle

### 1.4 Community Expectations

By using Dayo, you agree to:

- ✅ Follow all guidelines in this document
- ✅ Treat others with respect

- ⚠️ Warning

- ❌ Posts celebrating, glorifying, or promoting violence
- ❌ Graphic imagery of death, injury, or gore

- ❌ Graphic descriptions of violence
- ❌ Encouraging harm or dangerous behavior
- ❌ Posts about self-harm or suicide

```

## 5. SPORTS-SPECIFIC RULES & FAIR PLAY

### 5.1 Match-Fixing & Collusion

**Prohibited:**

- ❌ Agreeing with opponent to manipulate match outcome for money/advantage
- ❌ Intentionally losing matches for financial gain
- ❌ Throwing specific plays for betting purposes
- ❌ Colluding with referees or organizers
- ❌ Paying someone to lose a match
- ❌ Coordinated performance drops

**Examples:**

```
❌ PROHIBITED (Match-Fixing):
"I'll pay you ₱5,000 to lose tomorrow"
"Let's each win one match so we both qualify"
"I'll let you win this one, you win the next"
Three players coordinating to ensure specific team wins
Organizer accepting payment to make unfair rulings

✅ ALLOWED:
"I'm not feeling well but want to play anyway"
"That was a tough loss, better luck next time"
"Our team isn't clicking today"
Playing your best and winning
```

**Why This Matters:**

- Match-fixing destroys trust in the community
- Other players wasted time/money based on false results
- Undermines entire ranking system
- Illegal in some jurisdictions

**Enforcement:**

- First evidence: Immediate investigation
- If confirmed: Permanent ban for all involved + refund to affected players
- Law enforcement referral (if money involved)
- Investigation is thorough (not just accusations)

**Appeal Process:**

- Appeals allowed for match-fixing accusations
- Evidence required (communication records, pattern analysis)
- Burden of proof: Clear and convincing evidence

---

**Prohibited:**

- ❌ Intentionally playing poorly to manipulate rankings
- ❌ Throwing games to stay in lower division

- ❌ Intentionally underperforming for advantage

Examples:

- First-time minor rule break
- Unintentional violation

Action:

- Educational message sent

Minor: Accidentally posting off-topic content
Minor: Single spam message (not pattern)
```

---

Examples:

- Clear rule violation
- Second violation or pattern
- Moderate harm to community
- Intentional but not severe

- 24-48 hour suspension

- Clear warning about consequences
- Next violation: escalation

Examples:

```
Moderate: Harassment of one user
Moderate: Hate speech comment
Moderate: Second spam/promotion
Moderate: Intentional misinformation
```

---

**LEVEL 3 - Serious Violations**

Examples:

- Severe rule violation
- Third violation or concerning pattern
- High harm to community
- Dangerous behavior
- Predatory behavior toward minors (if minor)

Action:

- 7-30 day suspension
- Content removed
- Clear warning: "Next violation = permanent ban"
- Possible investigation

Examples:

```
Serious: Repeated harassment campaign
Serious: Sexual content (adult)
Serious: Third violation of any kind
Serious: Credible threats (non-physical)
Serious: Match-fixing investigation
```

---

**LEVEL 4 - Severe Violations**

Examples:

- Extreme rule violation
- Violence/threats
- Illegal content/activity
- Any sexual content involving minors
- Predatory behavior
- Hacking/fraud

Action:

- **PERMANENT BAN** (no appeal for most)
- Content removed
- Law enforcement referral (if applicable)
- Banned user cannot create new account

Examples:

```
Severe: Death threats
Severe: Sexual content involving minors (NO APPEAL)
Severe: Illegal drug/weapons content
Severe: Hacking attempt
Severe: Match-fixing with money involved
```

### 11.2 Enforcement Timeline

**Level 1 (Warning):**

- Report to decision: 24-48 hours
- Content removal: Immediate
- User notification: Immediate
- Escalation if repeated: Automatic (tracked)

**Level 2 (24-48 Hour Suspension):**

- Report to decision: 24-48 hours
- Suspension start: Immediate
- User notification: Immediate
- Appeals available: 7-day window

**Level 3 (7-30 Day Suspension):**

- Report to decision: 2-5 business days
- Suspension start: Immediate
- User notification: Immediate + explanation
- Appeals available: 7-day window

**Level 4 (Permanent Ban):**

- Report to decision: 2-5 business days
- Ban effective: Immediate
- User notification: Immediate + explanation
- Appeals: NO appeals (for minors/sexual content cases)
- Appeals: LIMITED (for other cases, high bar to overturn)

### 11.3 Progressive Discipline

**First Violation:**

- Generally: Warning (Level 1)
- Exceptions: If serious (Level 2-4 based on severity)

**Second Violation:**

- Generally: Suspension (Level 2-3)
- Escalation based on nature

**Third Violation:**

- Generally: 7-day suspension (Level 3) or permanent ban (Level 4)
- Depends on violation type

**Pattern of Violations:**

- Permanent ban (clear intent to disrupt)
- No appeals

**Serious Initial Violation:**

- May jump to Level 3-4 (no Level 1)
- Examples: Threats, illegal content, predatory behavior

## 12. APPEALS & DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 12.1 Your Right to Appeal

**You can appeal:**

- ✅ Content removal decisions
- ✅ Suspension decisions
- ✅ Accusations of rule violations
- ✅ Match-fixing investigations

**You cannot appeal:**

- ❌ Permanent bans for sexual content involving minors (absolute)
- ❌ Credible threats to safety (very limited appeal)
- ❌ Established illegal activity (very limited appeal)

### 12.2 Appeal Process

**Step 1: Initial Decision Notification**

You receive notification:

- What was removed/suspended
- Why (which rule violated)
- What action taken
- How to appeal
- 7-day appeal window

**Step 2: File Your Appeal**

Within 7 days, email:

```
Email: dayo.ph.it.services@gmail.com
Subject: "Appeal: [Content/Suspension/Ban]"

Include:
- Your account email
- Appeal reference #
- What decision you're appealing
- Why you believe decision is wrong
- Any evidence supporting your position
- What resolution you seek
```

**Step 3: Our Review**

We will:

- ✅ Assign different moderator (not original)
- ✅ Review all evidence objectively
- ✅ Consider your explanation
- ✅ Make independent decision
- ✅ Provide response within 7 business days

**Step 4: Appeal Decision**

Outcome options:

- ✅ **Upheld:** Original decision stands (reasoning provided)
- ✅ **Partially Modified:** Decision adjusted (reduced suspension, etc.)
- ✅ **Reversed:** Original decision overturned, suspension lifted

**Step 5: Final Decision**

Our appeal decision is final:

- ❌ No further appeals available
- ✅ You can provide new evidence if circumstances change
- ✅ You can escalate to [Company Executive] if belief of corruption

### 12.3 Match Dispute Process

**Disagreement About Match Result/Calls:**

**Step 1: Document the Issue**

- Take screenshots of match/chat
- Note specific calls you're disputing
- Note time/context

**Step 2: Report Within 24 Hours**

```
Email: dayo.ph.it.services@gmail.com
Subject: "Match Dispute - [Match ID or details]"

Include:
- Match date and time
- All participants
- Specific decision(s) you dispute
- Why you believe it's incorrect
- Screenshots/evidence
- What resolution you seek
```

**Step 3: Investigation**

- We review match record
- We interview referees/organizers
- We review communications
- We determine if error occurred

**Step 4: Decision**

- Possible outcomes:
  - ✅ Ruling stands (decision was correct)
  - ✅ Ruling overturned (decision was wrong - result adjusted)
  - ✅ Forfeit (rule violation discovered during review)
  - ✅ Rematch (in rare circumstances of significant error)

**Step 5: Appeal Match Decision**

- You can appeal match dispute decision
- Same process as content appeals
- Different reviewer
- Final decision after appeal
